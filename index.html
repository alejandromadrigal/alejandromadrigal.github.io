    Front End vs. Back End Dev

    It was important to understand the principles of being a front-end developer vs. back end. I learned the different type of web developers; front end, back end and fullwebstack developers. HTML Hypertext Markup language plays a big role for web development process for both front and back-end developers.

    JavaScript is principally responsible for Front-End developers and is also responsible for a good portion of Back-End development as well. Front-end development deals with what the user sees on the web application “UI” user interface visual aspects of the web page. Back-end developers’ deal with what the user does not see and deals with servers and utilize databases to manage the flow of information. They also use Web Stack, PHP and MySQL.

    It was clear that in this field of web development it was beneficial to know and understand and have experience with both front and back end development. I understood that being knowledgeable in both fields makes you stand out as a candidate for a job and increases your opportunities in this industry.

    SEO for ecommerce

    I found Search Engine optimization (SEO) to be very interesting because it touched a lot of points that pertain to online search results and rankings. I quickly learned that it was not easy to make your website especially an ecommerce site quickly rank and easily found online. Some of these concepts not only work for ecommerce sites but also to others. It is important to keep content simple and use keywords desired for attracting traffic to your site. Content is important to have present on your site whether written, or selecting titles or headings.

    For an ecommerce website there are many things that could be done to keep customers coming back. Starting with making website easy to use as usability is important. It keeps customers interested and that is the reason why design is ultra important for SEO. It was clear that keeping usability and structure simple was critical things like; page titles keeping a maximum characters of 60 and keeping important keywords close to the beginning of page. Equality important was to keep page structure such as images, headings organized in order to understand context of site. Website headings such as h1 and subheading h2.

    Many concepts learned in SEO for ecommerce are very important when it came to help search engines explore your site(s); variables like internal links, anchor texts. SEO for Ecommerce I found to be very interesting because anyone with an idea and product(s) or services is able to maximize or start using and benefit greatly because of it and the SEO concept could be adapted to many other sites not just ecommerce as well.
